Designing intelligent kitchen machines with embodied AI.

YasFood is a personal research and engineering portfolio focused on the intersection of robotics, multimodal intelligence, and connected kitchen hardware. The site is structured like an academic homepage so future work can be documented as systems, experiments, and deployable products.

Current directions include VLA robotic manipulation for beverage preparation, AI-native device orchestration in kitchen environments, and practical hardware-software pipelines that connect sensors, motion, perception, and user intent into one coherent workflow.

Working Framework

A practical research stack for intelligent food and kitchen systems

The presentation here intentionally bridges academic language and product language, so the site can support both technical collaborators and future business-facing discussions.

System philosophy

Build embodied AI systems that can sense the environment, reason over user goals, act through hardware, and report state in a form people can actually trust.

Perceive clearly Multimodal state estimation across cameras, sensors, and connected appliances.
Plan robustly Task decomposition that can survive timing shifts, object variation, and partial failures.
Execute safely Control policies grounded in hardware limits, user supervision, and operational constraints.
